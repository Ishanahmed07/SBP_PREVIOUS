Departmenatal Projects

In order to get an early introduction to research, every graduate student is required to do two Departmental Projects. Departmental Project may be Theoretical, Experimental or Computational. These nomenclatures apply to the students from the Colaba Campus, students from other Campuses should follow the respective guidelines for the projects.
Students are expected to choose their Project Supervisors from within their assigned Department, but, in exceptional cases, may choose a Supervisor from outside the Department, subject to approval of their assigned Department and the Subject Board of Physics. h4>PHY-200.1 : Departmental Project I : Research Methodology

This is an introduction to research methodology. The topic need not be original, but should not be regular textbook material, such as would be taught in a Lecture Course or a Reading Course. During the project, the student is expected to learn basic techniques of research, including literature survey, critical study of existing results, writing a technical report and making a technical presentation.

PHY-398.1 : Departmental Project II : Research Inception

This is a small research project. An original component is required but may be waived if the work involved is highly delicate or difficult. During the project, the student is expected to be able to apply the skills learnt in PHY-398.1 without significant intervention by the Project Supervisor. In normal cases, it is expected that the work covered in Departmental Project II will form the foundation of the first research project that students would undertake for their thesis post- PhD registration. Hence, it is advised that this project be selected with due consultation with the prospective supervisor.

Evaluation

For every Project, students must submit a Project Report in the proper format (soft copy is enough) to the Course Coordinator. Dates for these shall be as follows:

August semester : Last date for submission of the report : January 15
          : Last date for presentation : January 30
January semester : Last date for submission of the report : June 15
          : Last date for presentation : July 30

Every Project must be completed within the assigned semester and cannot normally be continued into the succeeding semester. The Project work assigned should be such that it can be completed within the duration of the semester. Extension of the last dates for submission and presentation (as above) shall not be granted, except in truly exceptional cases, such as medical grounds or major failure of experimental set up.
